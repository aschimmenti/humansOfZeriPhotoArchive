Zeri Photo Archive

Have you ever wondered what the history of a photographic archive is? Most probably it is the story of the humans who contributed to its creation and whose works have been catalogued. It will be the story of the men who travelled, studied and met each other to create those works.

And, in the end, it will undoubtedly be the story of the human being who made it all possible: Federico Zeri. Fototeca Zeri is the product of his studies in art history and at the same time the result of a broader vision, an invaluable resource for art history and photography scholars.

Fototeca Zeri: watch inside ARTchives

For a first approach to the Fototeca Zeri, ARTchives as a collection of archival descriptions of art historians’ collections is a resource we cannot ignore. And it is within ARTchives that our journey of discovery into the human history of the Fototeca Zeri begins.

Photographers in Zeri&Lode

In order to understand what was missing in ARTchives, we looked in Zeri & Lode. From this first research we were able to map a significant part of the photographers who contributed to the Photo Archive.

In this barchart we can see the ten first most relevant contributors in terms of quantity. The first thing to notice is that the largest contributions come from anonymous sources. We can also note that there are different entities, such as individuals, photo studies, institutions and so on.

People, Institutions and...

Given the nature of our investigation, we decided to focus on photographers from an individual and humanizing perspective, regardless of whether they belong to a studio or institution.

Therefore the rest of the analysis below will regard only the 28.1% of the contributors, with some exceptions...

The geography of photographers

In order to track down the roots of the Foto Archive, we wanted to see where the photographers originally came from and worked.

We can see most of them came from Europe, with few exceptions.
